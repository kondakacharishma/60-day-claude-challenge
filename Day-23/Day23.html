6.Key Customer Objections

  • "Free resources already do this." YouTube, ChatGPT, and senior advice are seen as free substitutes.
  • "I don't know if it actually works before I pay." No visible proof of outcome (offers, shortlists) yet.
  • Price sensitivity. Any recurring fee competes directly with tuition or family budget.
  • "AI advice feels generic." Fear that automated guidance won't understand their specific background.
  • Course fatigue / sunk-cost skepticism. Past paid courses that didn't lead to outcomes make them wary of a new paid tool.

7.Key Buying Triggers

  • Placement season begins (roughly August–March) and campus drives are announced.
  • A recent rejection, or a friend/peer landing an offer.
  • College placement cell recommends or partners with the platform.
  • Viral social content about resumes being auto-rejected by ATS systems.
  • An interview is scheduled in the next few days, creating urgent, high-intent demand.
Reading the pattern: objections cluster around trust and price; triggers cluster around urgency and social proof. The MVP should lead with a free, instant, visible proof-point (Section 8) before ever asking for payment.

8.MVP Recommendation

Table 8.1 — Build Scope Decision
QuestionAnswer
What should be built first?A single narrow wedge: an AI resume scorer (ATS + recruiter-readability check) paired with one AI mock-interview flow. Deliver manually/concierge-style for the first 10 users if full automation isn't ready.
What should NOT be built (yet)?The full 4-in-1 platform, a skills-course library, native mobile apps, gamified community features, vernacular-language support, or a job-board integration. These add build cost without yet-proven demand.
Success metrics≥60% of discovery interviewees describe the problem as painful unprompted; 10 students complete the manual pilot; ≥15–20% convert from free to a paid report; at least 1 college placement cell engages in a partnership conversation.

10.Pricing Hypothesis

Test a low-friction, one-time "Career Readiness Report" priced at ₹99–₹499 (resume score + gap analysis + one mock-interview transcript review) rather than a recurring subscription, since Section 6's objections show recurring fees compete directly with tuition budgets. Use this pilot price point purely to measure willingness-to-pay; a freemium + B2B2C (college licensing) model is the likely long-term structure once demand is confirmed. 13.Founder Action Sheet — Top 10 Next Actions

  • Draft an interview script targeting problem severity and willingness-to-pay.
  • Recruit 25–30 final-year students for interviews across 2–3 colleges.
  • Build a simple landing page around the free "resume score" hook.
  • Set up a waitlist form and basic signup tracking.
  • Manually deliver a concierge resume review + mock interview to the first 10 users.
  • Test ₹99–₹499 paid-pilot pricing with early adopters.
  • Reach out to 2–3 college placement cells for informal partnership conversations.
  • Resolve founder-fit gaps — begin a co-founder/advisor search for technical or domain expertise.
  • Track qualitative and quantitative signals against the Go/No-Go thresholds above.
  • Compile Week 4 findings into a written build/pivot/kill decision memo.
